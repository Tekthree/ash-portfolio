01

What you told us.

From the discovery call on June 29. These became the brief.

The goals

  • Attract high-budget residential clients
  • Impress potential employers at the same time
  • Move off Squarespace to a faster, more custom build
  • Book free 5-minute consultations directly from the site

The aesthetic

  • Classic with edge. Modern, elevated, luxury. Not boring.
  • Light, neutral background so the dark portfolio photos pop
  • Peachy or pink accent is welcome, but it can't compete with the photos
  • Mood boards shown full size, fully visible, never cropped
  • Navigation always visible on mobile. No hamburger-only menus.

Each project page needs

  • A proper header
  • A design concept statement
  • Portfolio images, big and visible
  • Before photos where they exist

Named inspiration

  • Michelle Dirkse (michelledirkse.com): white background, full-bleed photos, an editorial narrative per project
  • Plus the reference boards collected below

02

Where the current site falls short.

We went through ashriffeinteriordesign.com page by page. The content underneath is strong. The presentation is what's holding it back.

  • Mobile navigation is buried behind three dots
  • Half the projects have no description text at all
  • Galleries feel like template defaults, not curated case studies
  • No booking or scheduling anywhere on the site
  • No services page and no explanation of process
  • No pricing framing of any kind
  • The domain is long: ashriffeinteriordesign.com
  • The Squarespace template reads amateur against a luxury positioning

03

What the best designer sites have in common.

We researched conversion-focused guidance and top-performing interior design studio sites. The same structure shows up over and over: five core pages, each with a predictable set of sections that build trust in a specific order.

Home

The front door: style, credibility, and clear paths onward.

  • Hero with signature image + one clear value statement
  • Quick positioning: who, what, where
  • Featured projects preview (3 to 6)
  • Services snapshot
  • Mini designer intro
  • Testimonials or press logos
  • Process teaser (3 to 5 steps)
  • Final CTA band

Portfolio

The proof. Most visited, most critical for conversion. Quality over quantity.

  • Curated grid: 6 to 12 best projects only
  • Name, location, and type per project
  • Optional category filters
  • Soft CTA to start a project

Each project page

  • Full-width hero
  • Overview: location, type, scope, client goals
  • Design story and concept narrative
  • Gallery, room by room, with before & after
  • Scope of services delivered
  • Project-specific client quote
  • CTA: "Interested in a similar project?"

Services

Explains offers, clarifies process, and pre-qualifies clients.

  • Service cards: full-service, renovations, consultations, e-design
  • What's included and who each is for
  • Step-by-step process visual
  • High-level investment guidance
  • FAQs: timelines, contractors, scope
  • Primary CTA: request a consultation

About

People hire designers, not just imagery.

  • Positioning statement + philosophy
  • Story and background
  • Credentials, awards, memberships
  • Behind-the-scenes visuals
  • Values that differentiate
  • CTA to portfolio or contact

Contact

Turn interest into inquiries with minimal friction.

  • Reassurance: what happens after submitting
  • Form: name, project type, location, timeline, budget
  • Direct email and service area
  • Social links
  • Short qualification FAQ

Frequently added

Not required on day one, but common on mature studio sites.

  • Testimonials / press / awards page
  • Blog or journal for SEO and authority
  • Privacy policy and terms in the footer

05

The design direction.

Three references shaped the visual language. From each we took one specific move, and left the rest.

The type system

A lasting conversation

between past & future.

Cormorant Garamond for display. Arboria for reading text. The italic carries warmth, the caps carry authority. Her positioning in one typographic move.

The palette

Paper · #F6F1E9

Ink · #1A1614

Terracotta · #C07B68

Blush · #EDD4CB

Light and neutral so the dark portfolio photos pop, per the brief. The terracotta is the peachy accent territory from the current site, deepened so it holds its own without competing with the work.
